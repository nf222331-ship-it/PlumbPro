Pricing Guide

Standard pricing

Formula: hours × $100 + materials × 1.25 + $50 buffer, then any approved discount. A $150 minimum applies.

The hourly rate covers skilled labor and also helps absorb ordinary travel, fuel, tools, taxes, administrative time, and risk. The $50 buffer protects against small unknowns and forgotten costs.

Emergency / after-hours pricing

Formula: $250 response fee + hours × $150 + materials × 1.25.

Emergency work interrupts personal time, carries more urgency and stress, and often involves difficult conditions. Discounts are not applied.

Why materials are marked up

Charging at cost means sourcing, driving, selection, returns, replacement risk, and responsibility are provided for free. Supplier discounts are a business advantage—not automatically the customer's discount.

Why not every job is worth taking

The goal is not to win every job. The goal is to win enough profitable jobs. Consistently undercutting the market attracts price-focused customers, creates more workload, and leaves less room for mistakes or callbacks.

Why side work still has professional value

Customers are paying for the skill, judgment, safety, and completed result. The fact that the plumber also works for a company does not make the same labor worth half as much.
